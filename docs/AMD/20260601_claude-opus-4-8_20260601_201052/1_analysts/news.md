## 1. 📰 Executive Summary

AMD finds itself at a pivotal crossroads. On the other hand, Nvidia's explosive entry into the PC chip market with its **RTX Spark** chip — unveiled at Computex Taipei 2026 — poses a direct, near-term competitive threat to AMD's Ryzen desktop/laptop CPU business and has caused a notable selloff in AMD and Intel shares on June 1, 2026.

## 2. 🖥️ AMD-Specific Developments

### 2.1 Nvidia's RTX Spark: A Direct Competitive Threat to AMD's PC Business
The most impactful news of the week for AMD is Nvidia's unveiling of the **RTX Spark** chip at Computex Taipei 2026. This chip:
- Combines a **custom ARM-based CPU** with a **Blackwell GPU** in a single System-on-Chip (SoC)
- Directly targets Windows laptops and desktops — AMD's home turf with its **Ryzen** lineup
- Is marketed as **"the most efficient PC chip ever built"**, optimized for on-device AI agents
- Triggered a **sharp selloff in AMD and Intel shares** on June 1, 2026, while Nvidia stock jumped and ARM Holdings surged ~16% to a record closing high of $408.85

This represents a structural competitive shift: Nvidia, previously a GPU-only player in PCs, is now directly competing in the **CPU segment** that AMD and Intel have long dominated. The market's reaction (AMD selling off, ARM surging) reflects a repricing of competitive dynamics in the PC chip space.

### 2.2 Mizuho Raises AMD Price Target to $615
Despite the Nvidia PC chip threat, Mizuho upgraded AMD's price target to **$615**, citing:
- Strong and building demand for **agentic AI workloads** across the CPU market
- AMD's **EPYC server CPUs** and **Instinct GPU accelerators** positioned well for enterprise AI buildout
- Positive outlook for AMD's data center business independent of PC competitive pressures

This analyst action confirms that **AMD's data center/AI story remains intact**, even if the PC segment faces new headwinds.

### 2.3 AMD Invests in DriveNets' $410M Funding Round
AMD joined as a **new strategic investor** in DriveNets, an Israeli AI networking software company that raised **$410 million** (total capital raised: $1 billion). DriveNets is focused on large-scale AI infrastructure networking. This move signals:
- AMD's intent to deepen integration with **AI infrastructure ecosystems**
- A strategic play to position AMD silicon within broader AI networking deployments
- AMD's recognition that the AI buildout extends beyond chips into full-stack infrastructure

### 2.4 Stock Position: Approaching 52-Week High
As of June 1, 2026, AMD trades at approximately **$516.10**, roughly **10% below its 52-week high of $527.20**. The stock has experienced a dramatic re-rate in the past year, reflecting investor confidence in AMD's AI pivot. However, the Nvidia PC chip news adds near-term downside pressure.

### 2.5 Anthropic IPO Filing & Broader AI Ecosystem Boost
Anthropic's surprise IPO filing — also announced this week — has provided a fresh boost to the entire AI ecosystem. AMD, as a key supplier of compute for AI model training and inference, stands to benefit from continued private-sector AI spending and potentially Anthropic's infrastructure procurement post-IPO.
