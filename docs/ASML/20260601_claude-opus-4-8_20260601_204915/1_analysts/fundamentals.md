## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

**ASML Holding N.V.** is a Dutch multinational and the world's undisputed leader in photolithography equipment for the semiconductor manufacturing industry. Headquartered in Veldhoven, Netherlands, ASML is the sole supplier of **Extreme Ultraviolet (EUV) lithography machines** — systems essential to producing chips at sub-7nm process nodes — giving it a near-monopolistic strategic position in the semiconductor supply chain. ASML also manufactures Deep Ultraviolet (DUV) systems, metrology, and inspection equipment.

Its customer base includes the world's largest chipmakers: **TSMC, Samsung, Intel, SK Hynix, and Micron**, all of whom are ASML's critical end-markets. Due to geopolitical sensitivities, export restrictions — particularly to China — are a key ongoing risk.

## 8. ⚠️ Key Risks & Considerations

1. **Export Controls / Geopolitical Risk:** The Dutch government, under U.S. pressure, has restricted DUV shipments to China. China represented ~25–30% of ASML's 2023 revenue; restrictions reduce this addressable market. Continued tightening of controls remains an ongoing overhang.
2. **Semiconductor Cycle Dependency:** ASML's revenue depends on chipmaker capex cycles. A broad capex pullback (as seen partially in 2024) can cause order delays.
3. **Customer Concentration:** TSMC, Samsung, and Intel are the primary customers. Any slowdown at these companies directly impacts ASML.
4. **Valuation Risk:** At 54x TTM P/E and a PEG of 2.42, any growth disappointment could cause significant multiple compression. The stock's 52-week range of $683–$1,654 illustrates extreme sensitivity.
5. **Q1 2026 FCF Seasonality:** The negative Q1 OCF (-$2.19B) is seasonal but bears watching if deliveries slow.
6. **Currency Risk:** ASML reports in euros but trades on NASDAQ in USD-denominated ADR form. EUR/USD fluctuations impact reported financials.
7. **High-NA EUV Ramp Risk:** The next-generation High-NA EUV (TWINSCAN EXE) is being ramped for Intel and TSMC. Execution risk in this transition exists.
