## 1. 🏢 Company Profile & Overview

Alibaba Group Holding Limited (`BABA`) is one of the world's largest technology and e-commerce conglomerates, headquartered in Hangzhou, China. The company operates across multiple business segments including:

- **China Commerce** (core domestic e-commerce via Taobao & Tmall)
- **International Commerce** (AliExpress, Lazada, Trendyol)
- **Cloud Intelligence** (Alibaba Cloud — leading cloud provider in Asia)
- **Digital Media & Entertainment** (Youku)
- **Cainiao Smart Logistics**
- **Local Consumer Services**

The company is dual-listed on the NYSE (ADR: BABA) and the Hong Kong Stock Exchange (9988.HK), giving it broad capital market access. As of the analysis date, BABA operates with a market capitalization of approximately **$300.9 billion USD**.

## 7. 🔭 Forward-Looking Indicators & Strategic Context

Alibaba is making an **aggressive multi-year bet on AI infrastructure**, evidenced by the near-doubling of CapEx (¥86B → ¥127B). The Qwen large language model series has been open-sourced and is gaining traction globally. Alibaba Cloud revenue growth is being re-accelerated. This investment is margin-dilutive short-term but potentially transformational long-term.

### E-Commerce Competition
SG&A cost surge (+47.7%) reflects intense domestic competition from PDD (Pinduoduo/Temu), JD.com, ByteDance/Douyin Commerce, and others. Customer acquisition and retention costs are rising. BABA is investing to defend market share in China and expand internationally (AliExpress, Trendyol).

### Share Count Reduction
Ordinary shares outstanding fell from ~20.5B (Mar 2023) → ~18.6B (Mar 2026) — a **~9.3% reduction** over 3 years, primarily through buybacks. While buybacks slowed dramatically in FY2026, this structural reduction in share count supports EPS.

### Dividend Introduction
BABA has now introduced and grown a dividend — ¥33.7B paid in FY2026 vs. zero in FY2023. This signals management confidence in long-term cash generation, though the timing coincides with negative FCF.

### Interest Income Windfall
FY2026 saw an extraordinary **¥87.5B in interest income** (vs. ¥20.8B in FY2025), which significantly buffered pretax income. This may reflect revaluation of financial assets or higher yields on the large investment portfolio. This is non-recurring in nature and should be treated cautiously.
