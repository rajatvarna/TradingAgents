**Investment Thesis**: The debate produced a genuinely balanced case in which neither side decisively won, which is precisely what the Hold rating is reserved for. On valuation and franchise quality, the aggressive analyst landed durable points: gross margins expanded 310bps (36.7% to 39.8%) during the most intense competitive war in Chinese e-commerce history — a fingerprint of merchant-side pricing power that cannot be faked — alongside ¥186B EBITDA, ¥1,024B revenue, and a 13.59x forward multiple representing a 45-60% discount to global peers. His stress test was instructive: even haircutting forward EPS 20% to $7.38 at a constant multiple lands near the $100-105 area, roughly the prior capitulation low. On earnings quality and near-term risk, the conservative and neutral analysts landed the more decisive blows: the ¥87.5B interest income (a fourfold jump from ¥20.8B) sits at ~64% of net income, and the conservative analyst's arithmetic was the strongest single argument of the debate — BABA sold ¥123.1B of yield-generating investments in FY2026, those assets cannot generate income in FY2027, the cash base has fallen from ~¥400B to ¥316.9B, implying a base interest income closer to ¥42-52B and a 27-35% net-income headwind from normalization alone. Critically, the neutral analyst exposed that the constant-multiple assumption in the bull's floor is flawed: an earnings-quality revelation triggers simultaneous EPS revision AND multiple compression, so an 11x multiple on $7.38 produces ~$81 — meaning the valuation 'floor' is softer than the bull claims. The gross margin tension was never fully resolved: the bull's two-sided-market explanation (merchant pricing power intact, marketing spend defending consumer traffic) is elegant but the conservative analyst's rebuttal — that merchant reallocation lags consumer-traffic shifts by 12-24 months, so expanding margins could be a lagging indicator immediately preceding reversal — stands unrefuted by data. The neutral analyst's synthesis is the honest landing point: 2.7% revenue growth could reflect either early erosion or market maturation, and only disaggregated core-China GMV at the August print will distinguish them. On technicals, the confirmed Death Cross, MACD negative, price below all major moving averages, and the May 13th session giving back 90% of gains on 40M shares confirm the downtrend has not reversed — but RSI at 37.68 approaching oversold and Bollinger Lower Band proximity at $122.97 create a real reversal probability that argues against panic-selling. Net: the bears won near-term and burden-of-proof; the bulls won valuation and structural-value. That standoff, with a bear floor of ~$105-115 against a credible $150+ recovery path, is not lopsided enough to justify either adding or selling.
