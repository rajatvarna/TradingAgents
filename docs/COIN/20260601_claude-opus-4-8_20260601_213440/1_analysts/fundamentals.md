## 1. 🏢 Company Profile & Overview

Coinbase Global, Inc. (`COIN`) is the largest publicly listed cryptocurrency exchange in the United States. The company provides a trusted platform for buying, selling, and trading cryptocurrencies, serving retail users, institutional investors, and developers. Its business lines include:
- **Transaction Revenue**: Fees earned on crypto trades (primary revenue driver)
- **Subscription & Services Revenue**: Including staking, Coinbase One, Coinbase Cloud, and custodial services
- **Other Revenue**: Includes interest income, blockchain rewards, and lending

Coinbase has significantly diversified its business beyond pure trading fees over recent years, though revenue remains highly correlated with crypto market conditions (Bitcoin price, trading volumes, and market sentiment). As of the analysis date, the company has made significant acquisitions — including Deribit and other entities — which boosted its goodwill from ~$1.14B to over $4.2B as of Q1 2026.

## 7. 🔍 Key Risk Factors

1. **Crypto Market Cyclicality**: Revenue remains highly dependent on crypto trading volumes and asset prices. A bear market causes rapid revenue declines (demonstrated in Q1 2026 and 2022)
2. **Regulatory Risk**: Ongoing global regulatory scrutiny of crypto exchanges (SEC, CFTC, EU MiCA) creates potential compliance costs, licensing delays, or operational restrictions
3. **High Beta / Volatility**: Beta of 3.38 means COIN amplifies market moves 3.4x — significant downside risk in risk-off environments
4. **Goodwill Impairment Risk**: $4.21B in goodwill (mainly from Deribit and other acquisitions) is subject to impairment testing if acquired businesses underperform
5. **Elevated Debt**: Total debt of ~$7.96B (more than doubled in ~18 months) increases financial leverage and interest burden
6. **Mark-to-Market Volatility**: COIN holds substantial crypto assets on its balance sheet and in investment portfolios; fair value changes directly impact reported P&L, creating misleading swings in net income
7. **Rising Operating Costs**: R&D costs up 48% YoY in Q1 2026, reflecting aggressive talent and technology investment that may weigh on near-term margins
