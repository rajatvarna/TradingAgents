## 🏗️ The Trough Earnings Argument: The Most Dangerous Reasoning in Cyclical Investing

The statement is sometimes true — **when you can correctly identify the trough.** The problem is that every major value trap in financial history was bought by investors who believed they were buying at the trough. Lehman Brothers was a "trough" buy in February 2008. Bed Bath & Beyond was a "trough" buy in 2022. WeWork was a "trough" buy after its failed IPO. In each case, investors correctly identified that the business was performing below its historical peak — and incorrectly concluded that the trough had been reached.

**The questions you must answer before applying trough-earnings valuation methodology are:**
1. Is the earnings decline temporary or structural?
2. Is the recovery inevitable or conditional?
3. Is the current price already reflecting a full recovery?
