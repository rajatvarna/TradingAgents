**Recommendation**: Overweight

**Rationale**: For CRDO, the bull side won the fundamental debate, but the bear side won the near-term entry discipline debate. The positives are unusually strong: CRDO is showing a genuine AI data-center connectivity inflection, with revenue acceleration, high-60% gross margins in the latest detailed quarter, mid-30% operating margins, strong free cash flow, minimal debt, and a large net-cash balance sheet. The company is not just trading on an AI label; its active electrical cables, retimers, optical DSPs, SerDes IP, and ZeroFlap AEC positioning give it direct exposure to the bandwidth and power-efficiency bottleneck inside AI clusters. That said, the bear case cannot be dismissed: CRDO is priced for a lot of success at roughly 24x run-rate EV/sales and about 41x forward earnings, the stock has been technically extended after a sharp move, and any gross-margin compression, inventory misread, dilution, or analyst-estimate disappointment could cause a large drawdown. I do not think the evidence is balanced enough for Hold because CRDO’s execution, profitability, balance sheet, and AI infrastructure exposure are too strong to treat as neutral. But I also would not rate CRDO a full Buy at current levels because valuation and tactical overextension reduce the margin of safety. The resulting stance is Overweight: constructive, keep or build exposure, but do it gradually and with strict risk controls rather than chasing a full position.

**Strategic Actions**: For CRDO, maintain a core long bias and gradually increase exposure rather than buying a full allocation at once. Existing holders should keep a core position but trim only if CRDO has become oversized after the parabolic move; use trims for risk management, not because the thesis is broken. New money should be staged: initiate or add a partial position on constructive pullbacks toward the ~$210 area near the 10 EMA, add more only if CRDO stabilizes around the ~$196 VWMA/support zone, and consider a smaller momentum add only if CRDO breaks and holds above the ~$240.81 recent-high area with strong volume. Keep position sizing below a normal full-size high-conviction semiconductor allocation because CRDO has elevated ATR and beta; target a moderate overweight, not an aggressive max position. Risk controls should key off sustained breaks below ~$196, accelerating gross-margin compression below the mid-60% range, inventory days continuing to rise without revenue conversion, slowing revenue growth, worsening dilution/SBC, or negative estimate revisions. If those appear, reduce CRDO back toward market weight or underweight.
