**Rationale**: Alright, let me cut through this one because both sides argued well and there's a lot of noise to filter.

The bull's strongest, most durable points: this is a genuinely re-rated business, not a speculative story. $113.5B revenue, +39.5% in the most recent quarter, $8.55B FCF (up 357%), guidance raised, and — critically — the demand was cross-validated independently by HPE's blowout the same week. That HPE confirmation matters a lot to me; it answers the single most important question (is this company-specific hype or structural sector demand?) with "structural." The buyback machine (~6% of shares annually) plus $11.5B cash provides a real mechanical floor. And the forward multiple of ~23.5x is not absurd for a confirmed AI-infrastructure beneficiary.

The bear's strongest, most durable points — and they're real, which is why I'm not going to full Buy: (1) The gross margin trajectory is actually declining (23.9% → 22.3% → 20.0%) as AI servers grow in the mix. The bull's operating-leverage rebuttal is correct that net income still grew, but the bear is right that AI servers are the thinnest-margin segment and the leverage mechanism faces structural limits. (2) The FCF quality issue is legitimate — a meaningful chunk of that $8.55B came from a $12.8B AP working-capital tailwind that does not repeat at the same magnitude once revenue growth normalizes. Even the bull conceded a "normalized" figure closer to $5-6B. (3) The Nvidia single-supplier dependency is correctly framed as a dependency, not a moat. (4) The technical setup is genuinely extreme — RSI 89, $63 above the upper Bollinger Band, 232% YTD, record-volume climax — and base rates for parabolic large-caps do favor a meaningful drawdown.

Here's where I land. The bear essentially won the *price/timing* argument and lost the *business* argument. Notice that the bull retreated from "buy at $420" to "scale in at $370-400, full position $340-370" — that's a tacit admission that current spot is not the entry. But the bear's $275-330 re-entry requires a specific negative catalyst sequence (sharp deceleration + multiple compression) that directly contradicts just-raised guidance and competitor-confirmed demand. Waiting for that level risks missing a structurally validated growth story.

So the synthesis: the fundamental thesis is strong enough to warrant a constructive, accumulating stance — that's why this isn't a Hold. But the valuation is full, the margins are softening, the FCF is flattered by working capital, and the technicals are at genuine exhaustion extremes — which is why this isn't a Buy. Overweight with disciplined, patient accumulation on weakness is the honest answer. Build the position into the volatility the bear correctly identifies, rather than chasing the parabolic spot price.
