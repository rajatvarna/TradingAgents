On the structural under-allocation risk that both Aggressive and Neutral keep emphasizing — I want to challenge the framing that under-allocation is unbounded while entry-timing risk is bounded. That asymmetry only holds if you assume the missed upside compounds and the realized loss recovers. But realized losses from a thesis-break scenario don't recover within the position — they require redeployment of capital into different opportunities, which takes time and usually involves opportunity cost during the recovery period. And the "missed upside" framing assumes the trader has no other ways to express healthcare or growth exposure. The firm has a portfolio. If LLY runs to $1,300 without retracing and the trader's Tranche 1 is at 0.8% rather than 1.35%, the portfolio also captured upside in other healthcare names, in IHE inflows, in correlated mega-cap growth positions. The "permanent under-allocation" framing only works if you treat each position as an island, which isn't how portfolios actually compound.
