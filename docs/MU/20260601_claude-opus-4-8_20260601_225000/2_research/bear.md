## 📊 The Forward P/E Argument: Why "9.85x" Is Not the Simple Value Proposition the Bull Claims

My bull colleague repeats the "9.85x forward P/E" argument like a mantra. I want to spend a moment on why forward P/E multiples for **highly cyclical companies at earnings peaks** are systematically misleading — and this is well-documented in academic finance literature.

The concept is called the **"Fooled by Cyclicality" trap**. Here's how it works: at the peak of a semiconductor cycle, earnings are at their highest, forward estimates are extrapolating that peak forward, and therefore forward P/E multiples appear historically low. This makes cyclical stocks appear cheap precisely when they are most expensive. The same phenomenon occurs in steel companies, oil companies, shipping companies — any business where earnings are highly cyclical.

The correct valuation tool for a cyclical business is **normalized earnings** — what earnings look like through the full cycle, not at the peak. If we normalize Micron's earnings through the cycle — averaging the extraordinary current quarter with the $5.83 billion loss in FY2023 — the normalized P/E is dramatically higher than 9.85x. 

The bull's $105 EPS estimate assumes that peak earnings are now permanent. **The entire history of the memory industry says they are not.** I'm not saying earnings will collapse back to FY2023 levels. I'm saying that pricing MU at peak earnings with a "cheap" forward multiple is exactly the valuation error that destroys value for investors who enter at the wrong moment in the cycle.
