**Investment Thesis**: The debate produced rare convergence on the core fact: every analyst — bull, bear, and neutral — agreed NFLX is a fundamentally excellent business at a fair-to-modestly-discounted valuation, and that the long-term direction is higher. The structural case is undisputed and durable: normalized operating income of ~$3.96B growing 18.2% YoY (Q1 2026 vs $3.35B Q1 2025), best-in-class operating margins of 32.3% expanding from 17.8% in 2022, gross margins at a record 51.9%, revenue growth of ~16.3% at $45B scale, content amortization efficiency improving four straight years (45.4% to 35.2% of revenue), net debt collapsing from $9.2B to $2.1B, and a $9B/year buyback program. None of these rest on the contested headline figures.

The bear's single most valuable contribution was identifying the $2.85B non-operating income as the Warner Bros. breakup fee — a genuinely non-recurring item. This was decisive in deflating the headline FCF narrative: normalized quarterly operating cash flow is ~$2.2-2.5B (in line with Q1 2025, annualizing to $9-10B), not an accelerating $20B+ machine. This means NFLX trades at ~36-40x normalized current-year FCF (premium, not cheap) but ~26-30x forward FCF on a decelerated 15-20% growth path (fair-to-attractive for the quality tier). The honest synthesis, which the neutral analyst landed, is fair value for the quality — warranting active exposure, not urgency in either direction.

On position sizing, the aggressive 40-50% first tranche overreached on three fronts the bear and neutral exposed: (1) the 15% stop-out probability was asserted, and the 'dynamic stop' rescue is circular — it assumes recovery-before-grinding when every technical indicator (price below declining 50 SMA, RSI failed to reclaim 50 and rolled to 37, MACD at -1.66, declining Bollinger structure with price near the lower band) points to the grinding path as the near-term base case, in which the stop becomes MORE binding; (2) the correlated risk-clustering point is correct — in a stressed macro environment (live U.S.-Iran conflict, sticky inflation, elevated oil, 1.55 beta), the World Cup engagement headwind, ad CPM risk, SAG-AFTRA/InterPositive friction, and non-recurrence of the breakup fee tend to manifest together, fattening the downside tail; (3) a 10% S&P correction at 1.55 beta lands NFLX at ~$72.67, below the stop, on a normal-frequency event. Conversely, the bear's 25-30% first tranche calibrated triggers too tightly: in the most likely moderate outcome (stock grinds to ~$81, Q2 grows ~14%, recovers to $88-92), neither the strict 15% earnings trigger nor the $93 SMA trigger nor the $75-78 zone activates, stranding 70-75% of intended capital through the recovery. The 30-35% first tranche, with a 13%+ normalized-growth Q2 trigger, resolves both flaws — meaningful conviction expression with manageable absolute dollar risk and execution discipline preserved.

InterPositive is treated as a free call option with a modest contingent labor-negotiation cost — mildly positive in the entry price, not a present-day valuation driver and not an existential threat. The asymmetry over the holding period favors accumulating an excellent business at fair value, while respecting the confirmed downtrend and correlated near-term risks that argue against backing up the truck at a single price.
