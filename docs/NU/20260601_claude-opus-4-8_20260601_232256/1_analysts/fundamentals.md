## 1. Company Profile & Overview

**Nu Holdings Ltd.** is the parent company of Nubank, Latin America's largest digital financial services platform. Founded in 2013 and headquartered in the Cayman Islands with primary operations in Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia, Nu operates a fully digital, technology-driven banking model, eliminating traditional branch infrastructure. Its product suite includes credit cards, digital savings accounts, personal loans, insurance, investments, and payroll services. Nu went public on the NYSE in December 2021 (ticker: **NU**) and has grown to serve hundreds of millions of customers across Latin America, becoming one of the world's largest digital banks by customer count.

## 7. Risk Factors & Considerations

1. **FX & Macro Risk:** Nu primarily operates in Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia. Brazilian Real (BRL) depreciation or regional macroeconomic deterioration could materially impact USD-reported results. The effect of exchange rate changes on cash was +$286M in Q1 2026 but has been volatile.
2. **Credit Risk / Non-Performing Loans:** While the allowance coverage ratio appears stable (~17%), the loan book is growing rapidly in emerging markets. Economic slowdown or unemployment spikes in Brazil could elevate default rates.
3. **Regulatory Risk:** Latin American fintech regulation is evolving. Changes in Brazilian Central Bank policies (interest rate caps, capital adequacy requirements) could constrain profitability.
4. **Rising Short-Term Debt:** Current debt jumped from $351.5M (Q1 2025) to $1.87B (Q1 2026), a ~432% increase. While manageable given liquidity, the pace of debt growth warrants close monitoring.
5. **Competitive Landscape:** Itaú, Bradesco, and global fintechs are investing heavily in digital, potentially narrowing Nu's competitive moat over time.
6. **Tax Rate Volatility:** Effective tax rate swung from ~29.9% in Q1 2025 to only **8.7% in Q1 2026**, significantly boosting net income in the most recent quarter. Normalization of the tax rate could reduce reported earnings going forward. This is an important caveat for Q1 2026 earnings.
7. **Stock Price Weakness:** Current trading below the 200-day moving average ($15.50) may reflect market uncertainty about near-term FX risks or macro conditions in Brazil.
