## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

**NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA)** is the world's dominant designer of graphics processing units (GPUs) and system-on-chip (SoC) units for the gaming, professional visualization, data center, and automotive markets. Its flagship products — the H100, H200, and next-generation Blackwell architecture GPUs — are the de facto standard for training and inferencing large language models (LLMs) and other AI workloads. Its CUDA software ecosystem creates enormous barriers to entry, cementing its competitive moat.

1. **AI Infrastructure Monopoly:** NVDA's data center GPU segment is the backbone of the global AI buildout. Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Amazon, Google, Meta) are committing hundreds of billions in AI infrastructure CapEx, with NVDA the primary beneficiary.

2. **Blackwell Architecture Ramp:** The successive generation of products (Hopper → Blackwell) ensures NVDA maintains technological leadership. Each new architecture cycle typically drives ASP (average selling price) increases and upgrade cycles.

3. **Software Moat (CUDA):** NVDA's CUDA programming framework has ~10 years of adoption lead time, creating deep developer lock-in that competitors (AMD ROCm, Intel oneAPI) have failed to meaningfully penetrate.

4. **Fabless Model = Capital Efficiency:** NVDA relies on TSMC for manufacturing, maintaining FCF conversion rates above 90% and avoiding massive fixed-capital buildout.

5. **R&D Investment Scaling:** R&D spend grew from $3.99B (Q1 FY2026) to $6.32B (Q1 FY2027), a 58% increase — signaling continued investment in next-generation architectures.
