## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

**Oracle Corporation** is one of the world's largest enterprise software and cloud computing companies, headquartered in Austin, Texas. Oracle provides database management systems, cloud infrastructure services (OCI – Oracle Cloud Infrastructure), enterprise resource planning (ERP), supply chain management, human capital management (HCM), and customer experience (CX) applications. The company operates across three primary segments: **Cloud Services & License Support**, **Cloud License & On-Premise License**, and **Hardware**. Oracle has aggressively pivoted toward AI-driven cloud infrastructure and multi-cloud partnerships, positioning it as a critical provider of GPU compute and AI workloads in direct competition with AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud.

2. **Negative Free Cash Flow:** FCF has turned deeply negative (-$11.5B in Q3 FY26) due to aggressive CapEx. This is expected to be transitory, but execution risk is real.
3. **AI/Cloud Infrastructure Execution Risk:** Oracle is building data centers at unprecedented speed. Delays, cost overruns, or a slowdown in AI demand could impair returns on this massive investment.
4. **Valuation Premium:** At 44.6x TTM P/E and a $713B market cap, the stock prices in significant future growth. Any guidance miss could trigger sharp price corrections (as evidenced by the 52-week range of $134.57–$345.72).
5. **Competition:** AWS, Azure, and GCP are all expanding capacity aggressively. Multi-cloud and specialized AI workload environments benefit Oracle, but market share battles are intense.
6. **Customer Concentration:** A handful of hyperscaler partners (e.g., Microsoft, Google) drive significant contracted revenue.
