## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

**QUALCOMM Incorporated (QCOM)** is a global leader in the design, development, and commercialization of foundational technologies that enable wireless connectivity. Operating across two core segments — **QCT (Qualcomm CDMA Technologies)** for semiconductor products and **QTL (Qualcomm Technology Licensing)** for IP licensing — QUALCOMM powers the world's smartphones, IoT devices, automotive systems, and PC platforms. The company is the dominant supplier of Snapdragon SoCs (system-on-chip) for Android smartphones and is aggressively diversifying into automotive, PCs, and industrial IoT. QUALCOMM's vast patent portfolio in cellular modem technology (3G/4G/5G) underpins a durable and high-margin licensing business.

### 🔴 Risks / Concerns
1. **Tax Provision Volatility**: Massive swings in tax provision (e.g., $6.09B charge in Q4 FY2025 causing a net loss; $5.14B tax benefit in Q2 FY2026) create significant noise in reported earnings. Investors must normalize for these.

3. **Apple Dependency Risk**: Apple's ongoing efforts to develop its own 5G modems threaten QCOM's single largest customer relationship, potentially a multi-billion-dollar revenue risk.
4. **China Exposure**: A significant portion of revenue comes from Chinese smartphone OEMs (Xiaomi, OPPO, Vivo). Geopolitical tensions and export restrictions represent a material risk.

6. **High D/E Ratio**: Reported debt-to-equity of ~55.98 (elevated, though partially due to share buybacks reducing equity base) is a leverage concern.
7. **Semiconductor Cyclicality**: QCOM operates in a cyclical industry; the FY2023 downturn (-19% revenue decline) illustrates the downside risk during inventory corrections.
8. **Competition**: Intel, MediaTek, and increasingly ARM-based custom silicon from phone OEMs challenge QCT's long-term market share.
