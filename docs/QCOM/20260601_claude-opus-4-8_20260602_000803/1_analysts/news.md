## 2. 🏭 COMPANY-SPECIFIC DEVELOPMENTS

### 2.1 Nvidia's RTX Spark & N1X — Direct Competitive Threat to QCOM
The most market-moving event for QCOM this week is Nvidia's bold entry into the PC chip market. At **Computex Taipei 2026**, Nvidia unveiled:

- **RTX Spark**: A superchip described as *"the most efficient PC chip ever built,"* combining a CPU alongside Nvidia's Blackwell GPU, targeting personal devices running AI agents.
- **N1X**: An Arm-based chip for Windows laptops, already confirmed by major OEMs including **Microsoft, Dell, HP, ASUS, Lenovo, and MSI**.

This is a direct incursion into **Qualcomm's Snapdragon PC territory**, where QCOM had been the dominant Arm-based Windows chipmaker. The market reaction was immediate and severe:
- **QCOM fell ~7%** to approximately $234 intraday.
- **NVDA surged ~5%** to around $222.
- Intel (INTC) and AMD also fell sharply.

Barron's characterized the move as Nvidia "derailing the chip rally" for QCOM, AMD, and Intel simultaneously. The competitive overlap is most acute for Qualcomm since QCOM had staked a significant portion of its PC growth narrative on being the *premier Arm-based Windows AI PC chip provider*.

- The entry-level push aims to widen QCOM's total addressable market (TAM) beyond premium devices, but this budget segment may be more vulnerable to a well-resourced Nvidia competitor offering a premium-performance proposition that drives OEM attention upward.
