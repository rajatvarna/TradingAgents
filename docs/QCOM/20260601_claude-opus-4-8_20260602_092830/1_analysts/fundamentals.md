## 1. 🏢 Company Profile

**QUALCOMM Incorporated (QCOM)** is a global leader in semiconductor and wireless technology, primarily known for its System-on-Chip (SoC) designs (Snapdragon), cellular modem technology, and licensing of intellectual property (QTL segment). The company operates across three main business segments:
- **QCT (Qualcomm CDMA Technologies):** Semiconductor products — chips for smartphones, IoT, automotive, and PC platforms
- **QTL (Qualcomm Technology Licensing):** Licensing of CDMA, OFDMA and other wireless patents
- **QSI (Qualcomm Strategic Initiatives):** Strategic investments and incubation

## 6. 📉 Risk Factors

1. **Customer Concentration / Apple Dependency Risk**: Apple has been exploring alternatives for modem chips; any loss or reduction of Apple revenues (rumored for future models) could materially impact revenues.
2. **Geopolitical & China Exposure**: QCOM derives a significant portion of revenues from Chinese OEMs (Xiaomi, OPPO, Vivo). US-China trade tensions and export control restrictions pose ongoing material risk.
3. **Licensing Disputes**: The QTL licensing segment has faced repeated challenges from OEMs and regulators globally; any unfavorable rulings could erode this high-margin business.
4. **Inventory Build-up**: Rising inventory levels ($7.37B in Q2 FY2026, up ~13% from FY2025) could signal demand normalization risk or future write-down risks.
5. **CapEx Acceleration**: The rising capital expenditure trend (from $214M in Q2 FY2025 to $533M in Q2 FY2026) signals a structural shift to more asset-intensive operations.
6. **Deferred Tax Asset Risk**: The large non-current deferred tax asset ($5.97B) is subject to realizability conditions; any change in tax law or profitability outlook could require write-downs.
