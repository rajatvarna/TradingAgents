And the bull is right that five months of sector-correlated EM de-risking is not the same as Tencent-specific fundamental deterioration.

The business is excellent (bull wins) AND the entry timing in a confirmed, unconfirmed-reversal downtrend with thin risk-adjusted compensation is genuinely poor right now (bear wins). The bear never actually argued the business is broken — his thesis was 'great business, wrong price, wait for confirmation.' That's not a Sell thesis in substance; it's a 'don't add aggressively here' thesis. And the bull's thesis, stripped down, is 'the fundamentals will eventually win over 12-24 months' — which is a reason to OWN, not necessarily to AGGRESSIVELY ACCUMULATE into a knife that's failed two rallies this year.

When the strongest version of the bull case is 'modest positive EV on a great business' and the strongest version of the bear case is 'inadequate risk-adjusted compensation until the trend confirms,' the honest synthesis is to maintain exposure, not chase and not panic-sell. That's a genuine balance, and that's what Hold is for.
