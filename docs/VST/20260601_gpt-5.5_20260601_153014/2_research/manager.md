**Recommendation**: Overweight

**Rationale**: The bull side carried the debate, but not enough for an outright Buy. The strongest point is that VST is already showing a real earnings reset rather than just trading on an AI-power narrative: Q1 revenue, EBITDA, operating income, net income, and EPS all inflected sharply, and the multi-quarter operating income trend supports the idea that power scarcity is flowing into results. At roughly 14x forward EPS with a PEG below 0.5 and meaningful analyst upside to the Morgan Stanley $212 target, VST does not look priced for perfection if the forward earnings path holds. The scarce dispatchable-generation angle is also strategically important as AI/data-center load growth stresses the grid.

That said, the bear case is not noise. VST is levered, capital intensive, has negative working capital, and Q1 free cash flow was much less impressive than EBITDA, with buybacks plus dividends exceeding free cash flow. The stock is also still below its declining 200-day moving average near $172.91 and has stalled around the $160–$165 area after a sharp rebound. Moss Landing, regulatory uncertainty, rates, and crowded AI-power positioning are real risks. So the right conclusion is constructive but disciplined: increase exposure gradually, not aggressively chase. The bull fundamentals are stronger, while the bear technical and balance-sheet arguments argue for tranche sizing and firm risk controls.

**Strategic Actions**: Move VST to an Overweight position rather than a full-size aggressive Buy. For a trader starting from neutral, build toward roughly 1.25x–1.5x normal position size in stages; for a concentrated book, cap initial exposure around a moderate 3%–4% portfolio weight until the technical breakout is confirmed.

Implementation: 1) initiate/add a first tranche around $154–$160 while VST holds the 10/50-day support cluster near $154–$155; 2) add a second tranche only on a decisive move above $167.40; 3) add the final tranche if VST reclaims and holds the 200-day SMA near $172.91, which would confirm the long-term trend repair. Do not chase a failed move into $167–$173; wait for confirmation.

Risk controls: reduce back toward neutral if VST closes below $154–$155 with weak breadth or volume; cut more decisively if it loses $150–$151, and reassess the thesis on a close below $147–$148. Monitor forward EPS revisions, free cash flow after capex, debt reduction, Moss Landing liability clarity, and evidence of contracted AI/data-center power economics. Upside reference points are about $197 on an 18x multiple of current forward EPS and $212 at Morgan Stanley’s target.
